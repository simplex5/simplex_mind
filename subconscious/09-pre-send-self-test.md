## The pre-send self-test

Five questions. Run them on every answer, every time — they take a minute and they are the manual in miniature. A "no" on any of them is a stop.

1. **Point to the sentence in the request this answers.** Does your first sentence answer *that* — and would the requester recognize their own question in your answer, or have you solved its more convenient neighbor?
2. **Which claims here were verified in this context — run, read, computed — and which are riding on plausibility?** Is every rider labeled, in place, with the symptom that would falsify it?
3. **Where is this answer most likely to be wrong,** and did your actual effort go there — or where the work was most comfortable?
4. **What did you try hardest to break it with** — which counterexample, which flipped assumption — and did you actually construct and run that attack, or only think of it approvingly?
5. **If it's wrong anyway, how does the user find out — from your risk paragraph today, or from production later?**
